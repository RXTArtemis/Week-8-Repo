Frosted Buttercream recipe

After baking the cookies, we get to the best part:decorating! Today, we will be using Frosted Buttercream to add a little bit more color and design to our cookies. This Frosted Buttercream recipe is super easy to make. It doesn't take a lot of ingredients and it can be stored away in the fridge for later use. When making this buttercream, keep in mind that things like sprinkles and food coloring is optional, but it can definitely add that special touch to your cookies. Let's get started!

Ingredients:
  • 1/2 Cup of Unsalted Butter

    • Note: cold butter doesn't cream well.

  • 2 Cups of Powdered Sugar

    • Powdered sugar is excellent for making frosting. Granulated sugar is too heavy for frosting and will not incorporate well.

  • 1 Teaspoon of Vanilla Extract

    • This aids in incorporating all ingredients and adds flavor.

  • 2 Teaspoons of Milk

    • Milk loosens our frosting. You can reduce or add more milk depending on personal preference and thickness of buttercream.

  • Food Coloring

    • This is optional but add a fun twist to our cookies. For brighter colors, use gel food coloring.

  • Sprinkles

    • Also optional, but who doesn't love sprinkles.

Instructions:


    Cream butter and powder sugar until its creamy.
    Add vanilla extract.
    Slowly incorporate milk, little by little until desired consistency is reached.
    Add 1-3 drops of food coloring depending on color preference.
    Store in fridge to reuse for other cookies.
    Sprinkles can be added in frosting or on cookie.
